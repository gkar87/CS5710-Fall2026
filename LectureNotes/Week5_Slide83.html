AdaBoost Algorithm

Core Concept

AdaBoost (Adaptive Boosting) combines multiple weak learners sequentially, where each learner focuses on the mistakes of the previous ones.
  • 🎯 Adaptive Learning
    Adjusts to focus on misclassified examples
  • ⚖️ Weighted Voting
    Better learners get more say in final decision
  • 📈 Sequential Improvement
    Each model learns from previous mistakes
  • 🔄 Sample Weighting
    Increases importance of hard examples

Algorithm Steps

1. Initialize weights: w_i = 1/n for all samples
2. For t = 1 to T iterations:
a. Train weak learner h_t on weighted data
b. Calculate error: ε_t = Σ(w_i * I[y_i ≠ h_t(x_i)])
c. Calculate learner weight: α_t = 0.5 * ln((1-ε_t)/ε_t)
d. Update sample weights: w_i *= exp(-α_t * y_i * h_t(x_i))
e. Normalize weights: w_i /= Σw_j
3. Final prediction: H(x) = sign(Σ α_t * h_t(x))

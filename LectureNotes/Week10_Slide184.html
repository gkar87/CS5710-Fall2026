Vanishing/Exploding Gradient Problem

📉 Vanishing Gradients

Gradients become exponentially smaller as they propagate back through time, making it impossible to learn long-range dependencies.

📈 Exploding Gradients

Gradients grow exponentially during backpropagation, causing numerical instability and making training impossible.

Solutions & Mitigation Strategies

🔧 Gradient Clipping
Limit gradient magnitude to prevent explosion. Simple but effective for exploding gradients.
🏗️ LSTM/GRU
Architectures with gating mechanisms that control gradient flow and preserve information.
⚙️ Better Initialization
Xavier/He initialization to keep gradients in stable range from the start.
🔗 Skip Connections
Residual connections provide gradient highways, bypassing problematic layers.
📊 Batch Normalization
Normalize activations to prevent gradient saturation and maintain stable flow.
✂️ Truncated BPTT
Limit gradient propagation to fixed number of steps, preventing extreme values.

K-Means Clustering

What is K-Means?

K-Means is one of the simplest and most popular unsupervised learning algorithms that partitions data into K distinct, non-overlapping clusters.
Core Idea: Group similar data points together

• Partition n observations into k clusters
• Each observation belongs to the cluster with the nearest mean
• Minimizes within-cluster variance
• Creates spherical clusters

Key Characteristics

  • 🎯 Objective
    Minimize the sum of squared distances between points and their cluster centers
  • 📊 Input
    Dataset and number of clusters K (must be specified)
  • 🔄 Iterative
    Alternates between assigning points and updating centroids
  • ⚡ Efficient
    O(n × k × i × d) complexity where i is iterations, d is dimensions
  • 🎲 Sensitive
    Results depend on initial centroid placement

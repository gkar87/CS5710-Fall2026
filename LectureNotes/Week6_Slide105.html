Choosing K: Elbow Method & Silhouette Score

📈 Elbow Method

Plot the Within-Cluster Sum of Squares (WCSS) against different values of K. Look for the "elbow" point where the rate of decrease sharply levels off.
📊 Click to see Elbow Curve
✅ Pros:
  • Simple to understand
  • Visual interpretation
  • Works for most datasets
❌ Cons:
  • Subjective elbow point
  • May not be clear
  • Only considers WCSS

🎯 Silhouette Score

Measures how similar a point is to its own cluster compared to other clusters. Ranges from -1 to 1, where higher is better.
📈 Click to see Silhouette Analysis
✅ Pros:
  • Clear metric (0 to 1)
  • Per-point analysis
  • Considers separation
❌ Cons:
  • Computationally expensive
  • Biased to convex clusters
  • Sensitive to outliers

Other Methods for Choosing K

Gap Statistic
Compares WCSS to expected value under null hypothesis
Information Criterion
AIC/BIC balance model fit with complexity
Domain Knowledge
Business or scientific constraints on number of clusters

Overfitting vs Underfitting

🔴 Overfitting

Overfitting occurs when a model learns the training data too well, memorizing noise and specific details rather than general patterns.
  • High training accuracy, low test accuracy
  • Model memorizes training examples
  • Poor generalization to new data
  • Complex model, small dataset
  • Training error keeps decreasing

🟡 Underfitting

Underfitting occurs when a model is too simple to capture the underlying patterns in the data, resulting in poor performance.
  • Low accuracy on both training and test data
  • Model is too simple for the problem
  • High bias in predictions
  • Missing important patterns
  • Training error plateaus early

🟢 The "Just Right" Zone

The goal is to find the sweet spot between overfitting and underfitting—a model that generalizes well to new data.
Too Complex
Memorizes training data
⚖️
Too Simple
Misses important patterns
Perfect Balance = Good generalization to unseen data!

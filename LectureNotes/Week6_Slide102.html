Clustering Overview

What is Clustering?

Clustering is the task of grouping similar objects together so that objects in the same group (cluster) are more similar to each other than to those in other groups.
  • 🎯 Goal 1: Maximize intra-cluster similarity
  • 🎯 Goal 2: Minimize inter-cluster similarity
  • 🎯 Goal 3: Discover natural groupings in data
  • 🎯 Goal 4: No predefined categories needed

Types of Clustering

📍 Partitioning Methods
Divide data into k non-overlapping clusters
Examples: K-Means, K-Medoids
🌳 Hierarchical Methods
Build tree of clusters (dendrogram)
Examples: Agglomerative, Divisive
🔍 Density-Based Methods
Find clusters of arbitrary shape
Examples: DBSCAN, OPTICS
📊 Model-Based Methods
Assume data from mixture of distributions
Examples: GMM, EM Algorithm

🎯 What is Gradient Descent?

Gradient Descent is an optimization algorithm that iteratively moves toward the minimum of a cost function by taking steps proportional to the negative gradient.
🏔️ Mountain Analogy:
Imagine you're blindfolded on a mountain and want to reach the bottom. You feel the slope around you and take a step in the steepest downward direction. Repeat until you reach the valley!
θ = θ - α · ∇J(θ)
θ = parameters, α = learning rate, ∇J = gradient

⚙️ Algorithm Steps

  • 1
    Initialize parameters θ randomly
  • 2
    Calculate cost J(θ) for current parameters
  • 3
    Compute gradient ∇J(θ) (partial derivatives)
  • 4
    Update: θ = θ - α · ∇J(θ)
  • 5
    Repeat until convergence (gradient ≈ 0)
🎛️ Key Parameter:
Learning Rate (α)
• Too small → slow convergence
• Too large → overshoot minimum
• Just right → efficient optimization

Mini-Batch Gradient Descent

The Best of Both Worlds

Mini-Batch Gradient Descent combines the stability of batch gradient descent with the efficiency of SGD by using small subsets (batches) of the training data to compute gradients.
θ = θ - α · (1/k) Σᵢ₌₁ᵏ ∇ℓ(hθ(xⁱ), yⁱ)

where k is the batch size (typically 32-512)
Key Insight: By processing multiple samples but not the entire dataset, mini-batch GD achieves a sweet spot between computational efficiency and gradient accuracy.

• More stable than SGD (less noise)
• Faster than Batch GD (fewer computations)
• GPU-friendly (vectorization benefits)
• Practical for most real-world applications

Choosing Batch Size

  • 🚀
    Small (8-32)
    Fast training, more noise, better generalization
  • ⚖️
    Medium (32-128)
    Good balance, most popular choice
  • 🎯
    Large (128-512)
    Stable training, efficient GPU usage
  • ⚡
    Very Large (512+)
    Maximum stability, may need large datasets
💡 Pro Tip: Start with batch size 32 or 64. Increase if training is too noisy, decrease if training is too slow. Powers of 2 work best for GPU optimization.

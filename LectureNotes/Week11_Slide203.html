Markov Decision Processes (MDPs)

What is an MDP?

A Markov Decision Process is a mathematical framework for modeling sequential decision-making where outcomes are partly random and partly under the control of a decision maker.
MDP = (S, A, P, R, γ)
Key Properties:

• Markov Property: Future depends only on current state
• Sequential: Decisions made over time
• Stochastic: Outcomes have uncertainty
• Discounted: Future rewards are less valuable

MDP Components

  • 🏛️ States (S)
    All possible situations the agent can be in
  • ⚡ Actions (A)
    All possible decisions the agent can make
  • 🔄 Transitions (P)
    Probability of moving between states
  • 🏆 Rewards (R)
    Immediate feedback for state-action pairs
  • 📉 Discount (γ)
    Factor for valuing future rewards

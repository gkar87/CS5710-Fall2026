Choosing k in k-NN

The k Dilemma

Choosing the right value of k is crucial for k-NN performance. It controls the bias-variance tradeoff and determines how local vs global the decision making becomes.
🎯 Small k (k=1,3,5)
Low Bias, High Variance: Very sensitive to noise and outliers. Decision boundary is complex and wiggly. May overfit.
🎛️ Medium k (k=7,9,15)
Balanced: Good compromise between bias and variance. Usually optimal for most datasets.
📊 Large k (k=50,100)
High Bias, Low Variance: Smooth decision boundary. Less sensitive to noise but may underfit and miss local patterns.

Selection Guidelines

  • 📏 Square Root Rule
    Start with k = √n, where n is the number of training samples. Good initial guess.
  • 🔢 Odd Numbers
    Use odd k values to avoid ties in binary classification (especially important for k=2, 4, 6...).
  • 📈 Cross Validation
    Use k-fold CV to test different k values. Plot validation error vs k to find the optimal value.
  • ⚖️ Class Balance
    For imbalanced datasets, ensure k is not larger than the minority class size.
  • 🎯 Domain Knowledge
    Consider the nature of your data. Noisy data needs larger k; clean data can use smaller k.

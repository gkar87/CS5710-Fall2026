Choosing k in k-NN

Small k vs Large k

The choice of k balances between fitting noise (small k) and smooth boundaries (large k). k=1 is most sensitive to noise, while large k provides smoothing.
k should be odd for binary classification
Avoid ties in voting

Trade-offs

📊
Small k (k=1,3,5)
  • 🎯 Complex boundary
  • ⚠️ Sensitive to noise
  • 📉 High variance
  • 🎯 Good for many classes
📈
Large k (k=10,20)
  • 📐 Smooth boundary
  • 🎯 Robust to noise
  • 📈 Low variance
  • ⚠️ May underfit

How to Choose k

  • 1
    Use cross-validation to try different k
    k = 1,3,5,7,9,... with CV score
  • 2
    Keep k odd for binary classification
    k odd = avoid ties in vote
  • 3
    Start with k ≈ √m
    if m=100, try k≈10
  • 4
    Consider class balance and data size
    More neighbors = more data needed

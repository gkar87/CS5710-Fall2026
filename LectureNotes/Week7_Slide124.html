🔮 Eigenvectors and Eigenvalues

📐 Mathematical Foundation

Definition: For a square matrix A, an eigenvector v is a non-zero vector that, when A is applied to it, only changes by a scalar factor λ (eigenvalue).
Fundamental Equation:
Av = λv
🎯 Eigenvalue (λ): The scalar by which the eigenvector is scaled
📊 Eigenvector (v): Direction that remains unchanged (except for scaling)
🔄 Transformation: Eigenvectors define the principal axes of transformation
Characteristic Equation:
det(A - λI) = 0

🎨 Geometric Interpretation

Why They Matter in PCA:

📈 Variance Direction: Eigenvectors of covariance matrix point in directions of maximum variance
📉 Magnitude: Eigenvalues represent the amount of variance in each direction
🎯 Principal Components: Eigenvectors become the new axes (principal components)

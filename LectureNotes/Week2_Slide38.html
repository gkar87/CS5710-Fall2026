Regression Evaluation Metrics

🎯 R² (R-Squared)

R² = 1 - (SS_res / SS_tot)
Coefficient of Determination - Measures the proportion of variance explained by the model.
Range: (-∞, 1] | Perfect: 1.0 | Baseline: 0.0

📊 MSE

MSE = (1/n)∑(yᵢ - ŷᵢ)²
Mean Squared Error - Average of squared differences. Heavily penalizes large errors.
Range: [0, +∞) | Perfect: 0.0 | Lower is better

📈 MAE

MAE = (1/n)∑|yᵢ - ŷᵢ|
Mean Absolute Error - Average of absolute differences. Robust to outliers.
Range: [0, +∞) | Perfect: 0.0 | Lower is better

📎 RMSE

RMSE = √[(1/n)∑(yᵢ - ŷᵢ)²]
Root MSE - Square root of MSE. Returns to original scale for interpretability.
Range: [0, +∞) | Perfect: 0.0 | Lower is better

Metric Comparison Guide

Scenario Best Metric Why?
Model Comparison R² Scale-independent, easy to compare
Business Reporting RMSE or MAE Same units as target variable
Outlier-Heavy Data MAE Robust to outliers
Large Errors Critical MSE or RMSE Heavily penalizes large errors
Model Optimization MSE Differentiable everywhere

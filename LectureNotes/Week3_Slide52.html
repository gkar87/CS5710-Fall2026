Distance Metrics

Why Distance Matters in k-NN

Distance metrics are fundamental to k-NN algorithm. They determine how we measure "similarity" between data points to find the k nearest neighbors.
Key Concepts:

• Similarity: Closer points are more similar
• Feature Space: Distance calculated in n-dimensional space
• Scale Sensitivity: Different metrics handle feature scales differently
• Geometric Interpretation: Each metric defines different "shapes" of neighborhoods

Common Distance Metrics

📏 Euclidean Distance
d = √[(x₁-x₂)² + (y₁-y₂)²]
Straight-line distance. Most intuitive and commonly used. Creates circular neighborhoods.
🏢 Manhattan Distance
d = |x₁-x₂| + |y₁-y₂|
City-block distance. Sum of absolute differences. Creates diamond-shaped neighborhoods.
⚡ Minkowski Distance
d = (Σ|xᵢ-yᵢ|ᵖ)^(1/p)
General form. p=1: Manhattan, p=2: Euclidean. Flexible shape control.

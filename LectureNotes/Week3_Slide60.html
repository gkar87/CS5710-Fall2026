💡 Key Takeaways

1
Probability is Fundamental
All three algorithms (Logistic Regression, k-NN, Naive Bayes) are fundamentally probabilistic, giving us confidence scores rather than just hard classifications.
2
Assumptions Matter
Each algorithm makes different assumptions: Naive Bayes assumes feature independence, k-NN assumes local similarity, Logistic Regression assumes linear decision boundaries.
3
No Free Lunch
Every algorithm has trade-offs: speed vs accuracy, simplicity vs flexibility, interpretability vs performance. The best choice depends on your specific problem.
4
Evaluation is Critical
Accuracy alone is insufficient. Understanding precision, recall, F1-score, and when to use each metric is essential for real-world success.
5
Practice Builds Intuition
Interactive demos and hands-on experience with real data (text classification, medical diagnosis, etc.) build the intuition needed for algorithm selection.

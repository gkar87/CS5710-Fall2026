Policy Gradient Methods

Core Idea

Policy Gradient methods directly optimize the policy π(a|s;θ) by gradient ascent on expected reward, without learning a value function.
Key Insight:
• Parameterize policy directly
• Optimize policy parameters via gradient ascent
• Works for continuous action spaces
• Stochastic policies naturally explore
∇θ J(θ) = E[∇θ log π(a|s;θ) · Q(s,a)]
REINFORCE Algorithm:
θ ← θ + α∇θ log π(aₜ|sₜ;θ) · Gₜ

Why Policy Gradients?

  • 🎯 Direct Optimization
    Directly optimize what we care about: policy performance
  • 🔄 Continuous Actions
    Natural for continuous action spaces (robotics, control)
  • 🎲 Stochastic Policies
    Can learn stochastic policies (rock-paper-scissors)
  • 📈 Convergence
    Guaranteed convergence to local optimum
  • 🧩 Differentiable
    End-to-end differentiable for complex architectures

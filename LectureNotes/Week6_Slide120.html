📚 Week 6: Clustering & Unsupervised Learning Recap

🌳
Hierarchical Clustering
Explored agglomerative and divisive approaches, linkage methods, dendrograms, and how to interpret hierarchical cluster structures.
🎯
DBSCAN Algorithm
Density-based clustering with core points, border points, noise detection, and parameter tuning for eps and min_samples.
📊
Mean Shift Clustering
Mode-seeking algorithm for finding cluster centers through kernel density estimation and iterative mean shifting.
📊
Gaussian Mixture Models
Probabilistic clustering using mixture of Gaussians, EM algorithm, and soft cluster assignments with uncertainty quantification.
⚙️
EM Algorithm
Expectation-Maximization for parameter estimation in latent variable models, convergence properties, and practical implementation.
📏
Evaluation Metrics
Internal metrics (silhouette, Davies-Bouldin) and external metrics (ARI, NMI) for assessing clustering quality and performance.

🛠️ Clustering Algorithms Quick Reference

Algorithm Type Best For Parameters Strengths Limitations
K-Means Centroid-based Spherical clusters k (number of clusters) Fast, simple, scalable Assumes spherical clusters, requires k
Hierarchical Tree-based Small datasets, exploration Linkage method, distance metric No k needed, interpretable O(n³) complexity, sensitive to noise
DBSCAN Density-based Arbitrary shapes, outlier detection eps, min_samples Handles noise, arbitrary shapes Sensitive to parameters, varying densities
Mean Shift Mode-seeking Natural modes, no k Bandwidth No k needed, non-parametric Computationally expensive, bandwidth tuning
GMM Probabilistic Soft assignments, elliptical clusters k, covariance type Probabilistic output, flexible shapes Requires k, local optima, assumption of Gaussianity

🏆 Key Takeaways

1
No single clustering algorithm works best for all datasets
2
Data preprocessing and feature selection are crucial for success
3
Always validate clustering results with domain knowledge
4
Use multiple evaluation metrics for comprehensive assessment
5
Consider business objectives when choosing algorithms
6
Clustering is often exploratory - iterate and refine approaches

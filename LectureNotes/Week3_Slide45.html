Maximum Likelihood Estimation

What is MLE?

Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) finds parameter values that maximize the probability of observing the given data. It's the principle behind fitting logistic regression.
L(θ) = P(y|X; θ)
Likelihood function
Goal: Find θ that maximizes L(θ)
This gives the most likely parameters for our data

MLE vs MAP

📊
Maximum Likelihood
  • 🎯 Maximize data likelihood
  • 📈 Frequentist approach
  • ⚡ No priors needed
  • 📉 Can overfit
🗺️
Maximum Posterior
  • 🎯 Maximize posterior
  • 📈 Bayesian approach
  • 🔧 Uses priors
  • 📉 Regularized

Deriving the Likelihood

  • 1
    Assume Bernoulli distribution for each label
    P(y|x; θ) = h_θ(x)^y (1-h_θ(x))^(1-y)
  • 2
    Take product over all samples (i.i.d.)
    L(θ) = ∏ᵢ h_θ(xᵢ)^yᵢ (1-h_θ(xᵢ))^(1-yᵢ)
  • 3
    Take log to convert product to sum
    l(θ) = Σ[yᵢ log h_θ(xᵢ) + (1-yᵢ) log(1-h_θ(xᵢ))]
  • 4
    Maximize log-likelihood
    ∂l(θ)/∂θⱼ = Σ(xᵢ(yᵢ - h_θ(xᵢ))) = 0

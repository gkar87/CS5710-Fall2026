Support Vectors and Decision Boundary

Support Vector Theory

Support Vectors are the training examples that lie closest to the decision boundary and directly determine its position and orientation.
Decision Boundary: w·x + b = 0
Support Vectors: |w·x + b| = 1
  • 🎯 Critical Points: Only support vectors determine the final model
  • 📏 Distance: Exactly on the margin boundaries (distance = margin)
  • ⚖️ Lagrange: Have non-zero multipliers (αᵢ > 0)
  • 🔄 Influence: Removing them changes the decision boundary

Mathematical Foundation

🎯 Decision Function
f(x) = sign(w·x + b)

The decision function classifies new points based on which side of the hyperplane they fall on.

📏 Margin Calculation
margin = 2 / ||w||

The margin width is inversely proportional to the norm of the weight vector.

🎭 Support Vector Condition
yᵢ(w·xᵢ + b) = 1

Support vectors satisfy this equality constraint exactly - they lie on the margin boundary.

⚖️ Dual Formulation
f(x) = Σ αᵢyᵢ(xᵢ·x) + b

The decision function depends only on support vectors (where αᵢ > 0).

Bayes' Theorem Review

The Theorem

Bayes' theorem relates conditional probabilities. It lets us compute P(y|x) from P(x|y), P(y), and P(x).
P(y|x) = P(x|y)·P(y) / P(x)
Posterior = Likelihood × Prior / Evidence

Key Terms

📊
P(y) - Prior
  • 📊 Class distribution
  • 📈 Before seeing data
  • ✅ From training data
📈
P(y|x) - Posterior
  • 📊 After seeing x
  • 🎯 Classification
  • ✅ Our goal

Understanding the Formula

  • 1
    Likelihood: probability of evidence given class
    P(x|y) - "how likely is x if y is true"
  • 2
    Evidence: unconditional probability of x
    P(x) = Σ P(x|y)·P(y)
  • 3
    Example: spam detection
    P(spam|"free") = P("free"|spam)·P(spam)/P("free")
  • 4
    Naive independence: multiply likelihoods
    P(x₁,x₂|y) ≈ P(x₁|y)·P(x₂|y)

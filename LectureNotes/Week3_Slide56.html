Bayes' Theorem Review

The Fundamental Formula

P(A|B) = P(B|A) × P(A) / P(B)
P(A|B)
Posterior Probability
What we want to find - probability of A given evidence B
P(B|A)
Likelihood
How likely is evidence B if A is true?
P(A)
Prior Probability
What we knew about A before seeing evidence B
P(B)
Evidence/Marginal
Total probability of observing evidence B

Intuitive Understanding

Bayes' theorem is about updating beliefs with evidence:

🎯 Start with prior knowledge
📊 Observe new evidence
🔄 Update your beliefs
✅ Get posterior probability
🏥 Medical Diagnosis Example
A rare disease affects 1% of population. A test is 99% accurate.
Question: If you test positive, what's the probability you have the disease?
📧 Spam Filter Example
40% of emails are spam. An email contains "FREE".
Question: What's the probability this email is spam?
🤖 ML Classification Example
In a dataset, 60% are Class A. A new sample has feature X.
Question: What's the probability it belongs to Class A?

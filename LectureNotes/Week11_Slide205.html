Policy Types: Deterministic vs Stochastic

🎯
Deterministic Policies
A deterministic policy always chooses the same action in a given state. It provides a definite action for every state.
π(s) = a
or π(a|s) ∈ {0, 1}
  • 🔒 Predictable
    Same action every time in same state
  • ⚡ Efficient
    Simple to represent and execute
  • 🎲 No Exploration
    May miss better alternatives
  • 🏆 Optimal Form
    Optimal policies can be deterministic
🎲
Stochastic Policies
A stochastic policy defines a probability distribution over actions for each state. It can choose different actions with certain probabilities.
π(a|s) = P(A_t = a | S_t = s)
Σ_a π(a|s) = 1
  • 🔍 Exploratory
    Can try different actions
  • 🛡️ Robust
    Less vulnerable to exploitation
  • 📊 Flexible
    Can express uncertainty
  • ⚖️ Balanced
    Natural exploration-exploitation trade-off
Policy Examples in Different Domains
♟️
Chess
Deterministic: Always play best move. Stochastic: Mix strategies to confuse opponent
🤖
Robot Navigation
Deterministic: Direct path. Stochastic: Add noise for robustness
💰
Trading
Deterministic: Fixed rules. Stochastic: Portfolio diversification
🎮
Game AI
Deterministic: Optimal play. Stochastic: Unpredictable behavior
🚗
Autonomous Driving
Deterministic: Traffic rules. Stochastic: Lane changing decisions
🏭
Manufacturing
Deterministic: Production schedule. Stochastic: Quality control sampling

Learning Rate and Convergence

⚡ Learning Rate (α)

The learning rate controls how big steps we take during gradient descent. It's arguably the most important hyperparameter to tune.
  • 🚀 Too High (α > 0.1)
    Overshooting - may never converge, cost oscillates wildly
  • 🐌 Too Low (α < 0.001)
    Very slow convergence - takes forever to reach minimum
  • 🎯 Just Right (α ≈ 0.01)
    Smooth, steady convergence to optimal solution

📈 Convergence

Convergence occurs when gradient descent finds a minimum and the cost function stops decreasing significantly.
Signs of Convergence:

✅ Cost change < threshold (e.g., 1e-6)
✅ Gradients approach zero
✅ Parameters stabilize
✅ Validation error stops improving

Stopping Criteria:
• Maximum iterations reached
• Cost improvement below tolerance
• Gradient magnitude below threshold
